# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers oversee all activities leading up to and following a release. They coordinate across developers, QA, documentation, and deployment teams to ensure that releases are delivered safely, on schedule, and with full compliance.

### Responsibilities
- Plan, schedule, and coordinate release activities across all involved teams
- Ensure versioning standards and release checklists are followed
- Manage rollback plans and communicate release windows to stakeholders
- Track release readiness and gate approvals before deployment
- Serve as the escalation point for release-blocking issues

### Goals
- Ship releases reliably with minimal disruption to users
- Maintain clear audit trails and compliance documentation
- Reduce release-related incidents through thorough preparation

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews with Quality Champion and development leads
- Release notifications and post-release summaries coordinated with the Communications Lead
- Escalation reports to Project Managers when blocking issues arise

---

## Quality Champion

### Role Summary
Quality Champions advocate for process improvement and enforce best practices across every phase of the project lifecycle. They work closely with the Release Manager and the broader project team to ensure compliance with standards and to drive continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Review and audit compliance with quality standards during each project phase
- Identify process gaps and propose improvements
- Partner with the Release Manager on release readiness assessments
- Coach team members on quality practices and tooling
- Track quality metrics and report trends to leadership

### Goals
- Continuously raise the bar on process maturity and product quality
- Reduce defect rates and rework through proactive quality gates
- Build a culture of quality ownership across all roles

### Typical Communication
- Regular quality review sessions with the Release Manager
- Process improvement recommendations shared with all project roles
- Quality metric dashboards and retrospective inputs for continuous improvement discussions

---

## Communications Lead

### Role Summary
Communications Leads are responsible for internal and external stakeholder communications throughout the project lifecycle. They prepare status updates, newsletters, and important project announcements, and act as the main point of contact for escalated stakeholder questions.

### Responsibilities
- Draft and distribute project status updates, newsletters, and announcements
- Maintain a communications calendar aligned to project milestones
- Coordinate release notifications with the Release Manager
- Act as the escalation point for stakeholder communication questions
- Ensure messaging is consistent, accurate, and timely across all channels

### Goals
- Keep all stakeholders informed and aligned throughout the project
- Reduce ambiguity and misinformation through proactive communication
- Build trust with stakeholders through transparent and timely updates

### Typical Communication
- Regular status update emails and reports to internal and external stakeholders
- Coordinated release announcements drafted with input from the Release Manager
- Ad-hoc stakeholder briefings facilitated in response to project events or escalations

---

## Change Agent

### Role Summary
Change Agents drive the adoption of new tools, methodologies, and process changes across the team. They support team members through transitions, gather feedback on the impacts of changes, and report findings to management.

### Responsibilities
- Champion the rollout of new processes, tools, and methodologies
- Develop and deliver training materials to support team transitions
- Gather feedback from team members on the impact of changes
- Report adoption progress and risk areas to management and Project Managers
- Coordinate with all roles to ensure adequate resources are available during transitions

### Goals
- Accelerate successful adoption of process and tooling improvements
- Minimize disruption and resistance during periods of change
- Ensure every team member has the training and support needed to succeed

### Typical Communication
- Training sessions and onboarding materials shared with the full team
- Feedback surveys and adoption reports provided to Project Managers and leadership
- Regular check-ins with all roles to surface blockers and support needs

## How all seven personas work together

The following scenario illustrates how all seven personas collaborate during a typical project release cycle:

1. **Product Managers** define the goals and acceptance criteria for the upcoming release and align priorities with stakeholders.
2. **Project Managers** build the release timeline, manage risks and dependencies, and facilitate coordination across teams.
3. **Developers** implement the features and fixes, write tests, and participate in code reviews to meet acceptance criteria.
4. **Quality Champions** conduct quality gate reviews at each phase, partner with the Release Manager on release readiness, and flag any compliance gaps.
5. **Release Managers** schedule and coordinate the release window, confirm rollback plans are in place, and obtain final approvals from the Quality Champion before deployment.
6. **Communications Leads** draft and distribute release announcements and status updates, coordinating the timing and messaging with the Release Manager.
7. **Change Agents** support the team in adopting any new tooling or process changes introduced in the release, provide training, and collect feedback to report back to management.

This collaborative model ensures clear ownership at every stage, reduces the risk of tasks falling through the cracks, and provides all team members with defined touchpoints for escalation and support.
